The Three Ashras of Ramadan

First Ashra • Days 1–10

The Ashra of Mercy (رحمة)

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ وَارْحَمْ وَأَنْتَ خَيْرُ الرَّاحِمِينَ

Ask Allah for His vast mercy. This is the time to seek forgiveness and open your heart to compassion — for yourself and others.

Second Ashra • Days 11–20

The Ashra of Forgiveness (مغفرة)

أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ رَبِّي مِنْ كُلِّ ذَنْبٍ وَأَتُوبُ إِلَيْهِ

Increase in istighfar. Allah loves those who constantly repent. Cleanse your record before the final stretch begins.

Third Ashra • Days 21–30

The Ashra of Salvation / Protection from Fire (عتق من النار)

اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي مِنَ النَّارِ

Intensify dua for protection from Hellfire and for Jannah. Especially focus on Laylatul Qadr — it may fall in these nights.

Sehri & Iftar Duas

Suhoor / Sehri Intention (Niyyah)

وَبِصَوْمِ غَدٍ نَّوَيْتُ مِنْ شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ

"I intend to fast tomorrow from the month of Ramadan."
(Say before Fajr ends – silently in heart is also valid)

Iftar Dua (at breaking fast)

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي لَكَ صُمْتُ وَعَلَىٰ رِزْقِكَ أَفْطَرْتُ

"O Allah! For You I have fasted and with Your provision I break my fast."
(Say right after adhan or when breaking fast – beautiful moment of acceptance)

These short duas connect your fast directly to Allah's pleasure. Repeat with presence.

🌕 Laylatul Qadr Preparation

لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ

The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months.
Search for it in the odd nights of the last ten days (21, 23, 25, 27, 29).
